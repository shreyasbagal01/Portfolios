How we structure social media management

Every engagement starts the same way: we pick three content lanes that the brand can hold long-term. Three is enough to feel consistent in the feed and varied enough to keep the creative team alive. We refuse a fourth lane until the first three are working.

The deliverable, simply

  • Monthly content calendar — 16–24 pieces of content per channel, mapped to your three lanes, planned 30 days in advance with hooks pre-written
  • In-house design + edit — graphics, reels, carousels — produced by the same team that briefs them, not outsourced
  • Brand voice document — written in the first month, refined every quarter, so every caption sounds like you and not generic agency copy
  • Daily community engagement — replies to comments and DMs within working hours, comment-leaving on relevant accounts to grow inbound discovery
  • Weekly mini-report — one-page WhatsApp summary of what moved and what we are changing
  • Monthly deep-dive — formal review of reach, engagement, top posts, content-lane health

Why "three lanes" matters

A clinic that posts only patient testimonials looks one-note. A clinic that posts (a) condition explainers, (b) doctor day-in-the-life, and (c) before/after stories looks human, expert, and trustworthy in three different ways. The algorithm rewards variety inside discipline.

Which platforms we cover

  • Instagram — feed + reels + stories + DMs. Our highest-leverage platform for clinics, restaurants, salons, fashion, real estate (visual showcase).
  • LinkedIn — founder personal branding, company pages, thought-leadership. Highest leverage for B2B services, consultants, founders, agencies.
  • Facebook — local discovery for service businesses, paired with Meta Ads. Often quietly important for older buyer cohorts in Pune.
  • YouTube — long-form authority. Best when you have an expert spokesperson who can speak on camera weekly.

What we do not do

We do not buy followers, we do not run engagement pods, we do not auto-DM, we do not "10× growth in 30 days" anything. Growth in our work is verifiable inside platform-native analytics, screenshots on file.

Frequently asked

How many posts a month do you publish?

Between 16 and 24 per platform on a typical retainer. We have moved away from "more is better" — the algorithm now rewards quality, depth, and consistency over volume. A clinic on 16 strong reels per month outperforms a clinic on 30 average posts.

Will you respond to my DMs and comments?

Yes, during working hours. Community management is a core part of every retainer. We respond to comments and DMs within 4 working hours, escalate sales enquiries to you immediately on WhatsApp, and keep a shared inbox visible so nothing slips.

Do you create the content or do I need to provide it?

We create it. Our team writes captions, designs graphics, shoots and edits reels (in collaboration with you for on-camera moments), and delivers content ready to post. We need access to your office or clinic for shoot days, typically once a month.

Can I approve every post before it goes live?

Yes. Every piece of content goes through a shared approval workflow (Notion or WhatsApp, your choice). You see the calendar 30 days ahead and can edit, kill, or replay anything before it publishes.

How do you measure success?

Reach and member-reached counts (not just impressions), comments-to-reactions ratio (a quality signal LinkedIn weights heavily), saves and sends (high-intent signals), and most importantly — enquiries and bookings tied back to social. We report this monthly in plain English.

Will my account look the same as everyone else's?

No. The voice document in month one is built specifically around your business. We have run social for healthcare, real estate, personal-branding clients, and a venture firm — they look nothing alike on the feed.

What if my industry has compliance constraints (healthcare, finance)?

We follow strict review protocols for clinics and other regulated industries. All medical content gets a second-pair-of-eyes review before posting and we keep a written compliance log.
