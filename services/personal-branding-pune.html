What a personal branding engagement looks like

Month 1 — the voice document

We spend the first month on positioning. Two 90-minute interviews to pull out your point of view, your origin story, your hot takes, and your enemies. The output is a written voice document — the source of truth that every future post passes through. Without this, ghostwritten content sounds generic forever.

Month 2 onwards — three content lanes

We pick three content lanes you can hold for years. For a founder: company-building insights / industry hot-takes / personal craft stories. For a doctor: condition explainers / clinic life / patient stories. The disciplined three is what makes the feed feel deliberate.

The publishing system

  • 3 LinkedIn posts a week (Mon / Wed / Fri at proven times)
  • 1 long-form essay a month on LinkedIn or your own blog (we publish to your site and syndicate)
  • 4–6 Instagram reels a month repurposed from the LinkedIn content
  • Comment leaving on 20–30 relevant posts a week — the unseen distribution engine that gets you in front of new audiences
  • Monthly insights report — what travelled, what flopped, what we are testing next

The "viral three" template

Across our work, the posts that break out share four traits:

  1. A named entity — not "a famous startup" but "Canva", not "a brand" but "Biscoff"
  2. A concrete number — "100 rejections" or "₹10 SKU" or "₹26 billion"
  3. A pattern interrupt in the first line — "Hot take…", "Rejection feels like…", "[ALERT]…"
  4. One quotable sentence — written specifically so readers paraphrase it back to you in the comments

Three posts on this template drove 54% of our founder's annual LinkedIn reach. The playbook is documented in detail on our results page.

— Questions

Frequently asked

Will the content sound like me?

Yes. That is the entire point of the month-one voice document. After two 90-minute interviews and ongoing voice notes, every piece of content reads in your register — not generic agency tone. We are ghostwriting, not branding-by-template.

How much of my time does this take per week?

About 60–90 minutes a week is realistic. Roughly: 30 minutes to record voice notes capturing the week's insights, 30 minutes for content approval, 30 minutes optional for replying to your own comments. We handle the rest.

Do you guarantee follower growth?

No, and any agency that does is lying. We guarantee work volume, work quality, monthly reporting, and a system you can audit. Our founder added 4,308 LinkedIn followers in 12 months, but Kirti Associates LLP saw their lift differently — measured in member-reach and inbound enquiries, not raw follower count. We measure what your business actually needs.

Will you post a comment that disagrees with me politically?

Never. We get explicit pre-approval on any post that touches politics, controversy, or named-person criticism. Your editorial line is yours.

Can I keep my profile if I cancel?

Yes — entirely. Everything we produce belongs to you. There is no platform lock-in, no proprietary template. You walk away with a voice document, a content library, and a system you can run yourself.

Will this help me close clients?

Indirectly. Personal branding fills the pipeline — clients see your content for 3–6 months before they enquire. The closing still happens on the sales call. Founders who treat this as "my LinkedIn closes deals for me" usually misread the channel.

Do you work with Pune founders only?

Mostly. We accept select clients from Mumbai and Bangalore. Pune-based founders get priority because in-person shoot days and quarterly reviews are easier.
