04 · Playbook

The Meta Ads playbook

Five operating rules behind the ₹58 blended CPL — pulled directly from what the campaign tables above teach you.

01
Geo-lock first, broaden never (for real estate)
Single-suburb targeting (Moshi, Charholi, Ravet) consistently produced 2–4× cheaper leads than Pune-wide audiences. For property buyers, the "where" is the buying intent. We refuse to widen the radius even when the dashboard suggests it.
02
3-variant creative testing, weekly cull
Every campaign launches with three creative variants. We give them 72 hours and a minimum spend threshold, then cut the bottom two. The 8,005-lead Charholi campaign is the third creative iteration of a campaign that started with two losers.
03
Regional video creative > polished agency walkthroughs
Bollywood-style campaigns averaged ₹74 CPL. Straight broker-style "Video Ads" campaigns averaged ₹139 CPL — nearly 2× more expensive. Regional cultural cues drive the difference, not production polish.
04
Form-fill ads, not click-throughs
Every campaign in this dataset is a Meta Lead Form objective, not a website click. Removing the landing-page step is what makes ₹24–₹50 CPLs possible in real estate. The trade-off is lead quality — which is why we layer on the next rule.
05
Disqualifying questions inside the form
Each form carries one or two purchase-intent qualifiers (budget range, decision timeline, possession year). Pre-filtered leads convert ~3× better at the sales-call stage. CPL goes up slightly; cost-per-meeting drops sharply.
01 · The challenge

From near-zero to 748,571 impressions in 12 months

Most agency founders shout about results they can't show. We wanted proof — on our own profile, in public, with screenshots anyone can verify.

Webbloom Digital had already delivered 1.1M+ verified reach for clients on Instagram, Meta Ads, and Google by early 2025. But the founder's own LinkedIn was telling none of that story. 12 months later, it became the single biggest discovery channel for new client conversations.

"If a marketer can't make their own profile work, why would a clinic owner or developer trust them with theirs?"

One founder, one platform, one calendar year. No paid promotion. The plan: pick three content lanes (marketing hot-takes, founder/startup stories, platform critiques), publish on a steady cadence, study what each post taught us, and let compounding do the rest.

06 · LinkedIn playbook

Five tactics, already running for clients

Each one extractable from the data above. Each one already deployed on Webbloom client accounts.

01
Three content lanes, ruthlessly held
Marketing hot-takes, founder/startup stories, platform critiques. Three lanes are enough to look consistent in the feed and varied enough to keep your own creative process alive. We didn't add a fourth.
02
Name the brand. Cite the number.
Every viral post mentioned a named entity (Canva, Biscoff, KPMG/Deloitte) and a hard number ($26B, ₹10 SKU, 100 rejections). Specific beats clever, every time, in the LinkedIn algorithm.
03
Engineer the visual first
Before/after splits, screenshot grids, two-column comparisons. The image had to win the impression while the caption was still loading. Plain-text posts plateaued; designed posts compounded.
04
Write one sentence comments can borrow
"Rejection is the first step toward clarity" reappeared in 80+ comments — readers paraphrasing the line back at us. We started writing every post with one "quotable" line built in. The 1,092 comments came from there.
05
Defend the baseline, hunt the spike
2–3 posts a week kept the algorithm warm. One or two per month were structured as deliberate spike attempts (hotter take, stronger visual, riskier topic). Most baseline posts did baseline numbers — and that's the job.
