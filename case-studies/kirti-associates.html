The starting point

Kirti Associates LLP is a Pune-based professional services firm with a strong reputation in its niche, built over years of word-of-mouth. The founder had a LinkedIn presence — sporadic posts, a couple of hundred connections engaging with most updates — but no system. The brief was simple: convert offline credibility into online visibility, without diluting the firm's voice or losing the founder's sense of self.

Month 1 — the voice document

We refuse to ghostwrite without a voice document, and Kirti was no exception. Two 90-minute interviews. We pulled out the founder's point of view on the practice area, the war stories from 15+ years of work, the contrarian opinions, and the patient/client philosophy that defined the firm. The document was 14 pages by the end of week three — and became the source of truth every future post passed through.

Month 2 onwards — three content lanes

We picked three lanes the founder could hold indefinitely:

  • Practice insights — short, plain-English explanations of how the practice area actually works, written for prospective clients rather than peers
  • Founder craft stories — moments from the firm's history, lessons learned, the human behind the practitioner
  • Industry hot-takes — sharp, named, specific takes on what was changing in the practice area (regulatory shifts, common client mistakes, AI disruption)

The publishing system

  • 3 LinkedIn posts a week (Monday / Wednesday / Friday)
  • 1 long-form piece per month, syndicated to LinkedIn + the firm's site
  • 4 Instagram reels per month, repurposed from LinkedIn long-form
  • WhatsApp Status updates of the highest-performing LinkedIn posts each week
  • Comment-leaving on 20 relevant posts a week — the unseen distribution engine

What we got right

1. The voice held

Six months in, the firm's admin staff couldn't tell ghostwritten posts from posts the founder wrote himself. That is the marker of a good voice document. Clients reading the posts heard their advisor — not a generic LinkedIn voice.

2. Specificity beat polish

The posts that broke out were always the most specific — a named precedent, a hard number, a recent and specific change. "A practitioner's view on X" lost to "Last month, three different clients asked me about Y. Here's what I told them."

3. Comments became the funnel

Comments-to-reactions ratio averaged above 12% for the engagement — a quality signal LinkedIn weights ~10× more than reactions in distribution. The comment threads became the inbound funnel: prospects identified themselves, asked specific questions, and moved into DMs.

"Word-of-mouth has always closed our work. LinkedIn just made the door wider."

Lessons portable to other Pune professional services

  1. The voice document is non-negotiable. Without it, ghostwriting sounds generic forever.
  2. Specificity is the moat. Named precedents, real numbers, real war stories. Vague posts plateau.
  3. Comments matter more than reactions. Write posts that invite a real comment, not a thumb-tap.
  4. Multi-platform repurposing is free leverage. A LinkedIn post → reel → WhatsApp Status doubles the impressions for an hour of work.
  5. Don't guarantee follower counts. The right metric for professional services is members-reached and inbound enquiries, not vanity followers.
